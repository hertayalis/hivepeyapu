Biography of Rudy Gobert

Rudy Gobert was born on June 26, 1992, in Saint-Quentin, France. He grew up playing basketball and joined the Cholet Basket junior team at the age of 15. Gobert quickly developed into a promising young player, standing at an impressive 7 feet 1 inch tall.

In 2013, Gobert entered the NBA draft and was selected as the 27th overall pick by the Denver Nuggets. However, he was soon traded to the Utah Jazz, where he would make his debut in the 2013-2014 season.

Gobert had a slow start in his rookie season, but quickly made an impact in his second year. His size and defensive abilities made him a valuable asset to the Jazz, and he soon became one of the top shot-blockers in the league.

In the 2016-2017 season, Gobert had a breakout year, averaging a double-double with 14 points and 12 rebounds per game. He was also named to his first All-Defensive First Team, and won the NBA Defensive Player of the Year award.

Gobert continued to improve and establish himself as one of the best centers in the league. He earned All-Star honors in 2020 and 2021, and won the NBA Defensive Player of the Year award twice more in 2018-2019 and 2019-2020.

Off the court, Gobert is known for his charitable work and has established the Rudy Gobert Foundation to help underprivileged children. He also famously became the first NBA player to test positive for COVID-19 in March 2020, which led to the suspension of the NBA season due to the pandemic.
