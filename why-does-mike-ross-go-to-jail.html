Why Does Mike Ross Go To Jail?

Publish date: 2024-07-12

Mike Ross, a central character in the popular legal drama “Suits,” faces a series of legal and ethical challenges throughout the series that ultimately lead to his imprisonment. His journey to jail is a complex interplay of his fraudulent past, moral dilemmas, and the high-stakes world of corporate law. This article delves into the intricacies of why Mike Ross, portrayed by actor Patrick J. Adams, ends up behind bars, exploring the pivotal moments and key decisions that seal his fate.

The Premise of Mike Ross’s Fraud

Before understanding why Mike Ross goes to jail, it’s essential to grasp the foundation of his fraudulent career:

  • Mike Ross is a brilliant college dropout with an eidetic memory, which allows him to remember everything he reads.
  • He starts working at Pearson Hardman, a prestigious New York law firm, despite never attending law school or passing the bar exam.
  • Harvey Specter, a senior partner at the firm, hires Mike knowing his secret, thus becoming complicit in the fraud.

The Discovery of Mike’s Secret

Mike’s secret of not being a legitimate lawyer is a ticking time bomb throughout the series:

  • Colleagues and rivals become suspicious of Mike’s background.
  • His lack of a legitimate law degree and bar credentials are eventually uncovered.
  • The threat of exposure looms over Mike and the firm, creating tension and conflict.

The Arrest and Charges

Mike Ross’s eventual arrest is a culmination of the discovery of his fraudulent practice:

  • He is arrested for conspiracy to commit fraud.
  • The charges are serious, with the potential for a lengthy prison sentence.
  • His arrest sends shockwaves through Pearson Specter Litt, the firm where he works.

The Trial

Mike’s trial is a central focus of the series, showcasing the legal battle that ensues:

  • Mike decides to represent himself, with support from Harvey and the firm.
  • The prosecution is relentless, aiming to make an example out of Mike.
  • Witnesses and evidence against Mike mount, increasing the likelihood of conviction.

The Plea Deal

Faced with the overwhelming evidence, Mike is offered a plea deal:

  • The deal requires Mike to plead guilty in exchange for a reduced sentence.
  • He struggles with the decision, weighing the impact on his future and those he cares about.
  • Ultimately, Mike accepts the plea deal to protect his colleagues from further fallout.

The Sacrifice

Mike’s decision to go to jail is a sacrifice that highlights his character development:

  • He chooses to protect his mentor, Harvey, and the firm from further damage.
  • His actions demonstrate a sense of responsibility and atonement for his fraudulent past.
  • Mike’s sacrifice is a pivotal moment in the series, marking a turning point for the character.

The Impact on Pearson Specter Litt

Mike’s imprisonment has significant repercussions for the firm:

  • The firm’s reputation is tarnished by the scandal.
  • Partners and clients question the integrity of Pearson Specter Litt.
  • The firm faces internal strife and the potential for collapse.

Life in Prison

Mike’s time in prison is a challenging period for him:

  • He faces the harsh realities of life behind bars.
  • Mike must navigate the dangers and politics of prison life.
  • His experiences in jail further shape his character and outlook on life.

The Role of Mike’s Past

Mike’s past plays a crucial role in his journey to jail:

  • His childhood traumas and experiences influence his decisions and moral compass.
  • His relationship with his grandmother and her passing have a profound impact on him.
  • Mike’s past indiscretions and shortcuts catch up with him, leading to his downfall.

Redemption and Release

Mike’s story does not end with his imprisonment:

  • He seeks redemption and works to earn his freedom.
  • His legal acumen is put to use helping other inmates with their cases.
  • Mike’s efforts to better himself and help others play a role in his eventual release.

Mike Ross’s Legacy

Mike’s time in jail leaves a lasting legacy on the series and the characters:

  • His experiences shape his perspective on justice and the legal system.
  • The relationships he forms in prison and at the firm are tested and transformed.
  • Mike’s journey serves as a cautionary tale about the consequences of deception.

Lessons Learned

The narrative of Mike Ross going to jail serves as a vehicle for several lessons:

  • The importance of integrity and honesty in one’s professional and personal life.
  • The impact of one’s actions on others and the ripple effect they can have.
  • The potential for growth and change, even in the face of adversity.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Mike Ross’s journey to jail is a multifaceted story of fraud, ethical dilemmas, and personal growth. His decision to accept the plea deal and go to prison is a testament to his character development and the high stakes of the legal world. Mike’s experiences in jail and the impact on those around him underscore the themes of redemption, sacrifice, and the consequences of one’s actions. “Suits” uses Mike’s story to explore deeper questions about the law, justice, and what it means to do the right thing.

FAQ Section

  • What season does Mike Ross go to jail?
    Mike Ross goes to jail in Season 5 of “Suits,” with his imprisonment continuing into Season 6.
  • How long is Mike Ross in jail?
    Mike Ross serves a two-year prison sentence but is released early for good behavior and his work helping other inmates.
  • Does Mike Ross become a legitimate lawyer after his release?
    After his release from prison, Mike Ross takes and passes the bar exam, becoming a legitimate lawyer with the help of a special provision and the support of his mentor, Harvey Specter.
